How the FIRE calculator works

FIRE stands for Financial Independence, Retire Early. The core concept is simple: accumulate enough invested assets that the annual returns from your portfolio cover your living expenses indefinitely — so work becomes optional. The most common benchmark is the 25x rule: save 25 times your annual spending, which corresponds to a 4% safe withdrawal rate.

This calculator has three modes. FIRE number mode shows your target, how many years to reach it, and the Lean/Regular/Fat FIRE variants based on different spending levels. Coast FIRE mode calculates the point at which you can stop contributing — your savings will compound to your full FIRE number on their own by retirement age. Savings rate mode shows how dramatically your timeline changes at different savings rates.

Lean FIRE, Regular FIRE, and Fat FIRE

  • Lean FIRE — retiring on a frugal budget, typically $25,000–$40,000/year. Requires a smaller number but leaves less margin for unexpected expenses.
  • Regular FIRE — based on your actual entered spending. The standard FIRE target for most people.
  • Fat FIRE — retiring with a comfortable lifestyle, typically $100,000+/year. Requires a significantly larger portfolio but provides the most flexibility.
