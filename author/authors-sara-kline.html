Sara started trading equities at 24 and spent three years learning the hard way that position sizing matters more than stock picking. After blowing up a small account by ignoring stop losses — a mistake she's written about openly — she rebuilt with a strict risk-first approach and hasn't looked back.

She writes about trading mechanics with the directness of someone who learned these lessons through real losses, not textbooks. Position sizing, stop loss placement, risk/reward ratios, and the psychological discipline required to follow a system when it's uncomfortable — these are the topics she covers, and she covers them without sugarcoating.

Sara doesn't offer trade recommendations or claim consistent profitability. What she does is explain the mechanics of risk management clearly enough that traders at any level can apply them. The rest is up to you.
