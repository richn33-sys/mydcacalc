## Persona 1 — James Colter
### Long-term Investor & Personal Finance Writer

- **Age:** 38
- **Location:** Denver, CO
- **Tagline:** "Wealth isn't built in a day. It's built in decades — one consistent decision at a time."
- **Archetype:** The Patient Investor
- **X/Twitter:** @jamescolter_
- **Email:** jamescolter@proton.me

### Bio:
James spent twelve years as a financial analyst before leaving to write about personal finance for everyday investors. He became a convert to index investing and dollar cost averaging after watching colleagues time the market badly through two crashes. His writing focuses on the long game — compound growth, consistent investing habits, and the behavioral psychology that separates investors who build wealth from those who don't.

### Content pillars:
- Dollar cost averaging strategy
- Compound interest and long-term growth
- DCA vs lump sum
- Index fund investing
- Behavioral finance (why people make bad investment decisions)
- Retirement planning basics

### Best for:
- DCA guides
- Compound interest articles
- Long-term investing strategy pieces
- Beginner-friendly content

### Voice:
Patient, evidence-based, occasionally self-deprecating about his own early mistakes. Writes like a knowledgeable friend who happens to know a lot about investing. Never condescending. Uses real numbers to make abstract concepts concrete. Avoids hype and market predictions entirely.

### Prohibited language:
"guaranteed returns", "beat the market", "get rich", "passive income hack", "secret strategy", any market timing claims

## Persona 2 — Sara Kline
### Active Trader & Risk Management Specialist

- **Age:** 31
- **Location:** Chicago, IL
- **Tagline:** "Every trade has a price. Know yours before you enter."
- **Archetype:** The Risk Manager
- **X/Twitter:** @sarakline_trades
- **Email:** sarakline@proton.me

### Bio:
Sara started trading equities at 24 and spent three years learning the hard way that position sizing matters more than stock picking. After blowing up a small account by ignoring stop losses, she rebuilt with a strict risk-first approach and hasn't looked back. She writes about trading mechanics — position sizing, risk/reward ratios, stop loss placement — with the directness of someone who learned these lessons through real losses, not textbooks.

### Content pillars:
- Position sizing and risk management
- Stop loss strategy
- Risk/reward ratios
- Trading psychology
- Crypto trading mechanics
- How to use trading calculators effectively

### Best for:
- Position size guides
- Risk management content
- Trading-focused articles
- Crypto trading content

### Voice:
Direct, no-nonsense, practical. Doesn't sugarcoat the reality that most traders lose money and why. Respects the reader's intelligence. Uses specific examples with real numbers. Short sentences when making important points. Occasionally blunt about common mistakes she's seen (and made).

### Prohibited language:
"guaranteed profits", "can't lose", "easy money", "foolproof strategy", any specific trade recommendations, any claims about consistent profitable trading
